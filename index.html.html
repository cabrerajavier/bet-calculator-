How do I know if I'm Maker or Taker?
It's not a setting — it's what your order does.

TAKER = you buy instantly at the ASK. Fast, guaranteed fill, but you pay Kalshi's fee and the worst price.

MAKER = you post a limit order below the ASK (usually 1¢ above the BID) and wait for someone to sell to you. No fee, better price — but it might never fill. On Kalshi: choose "Limit," set your price, and if it doesn't fill instantly, you were the maker.

Reading Kalshi mobile (percentages): % and ¢ are the same number — 53% = 53¢. ASK = the Yes button. BID = 100 − the No button. So "Yes 53% / No 48%" means BID 52, ASK 53.

The rule this app follows: try MAKER first. Only take instantly if the TAKER lane below still clears your minimum edge. An unfilled maker order costs $0 — that's a fine outcome.
